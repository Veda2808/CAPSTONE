About Water Footprints

The water footprint of a product or activity is the total volume of water used directly and indirectly to produce it. It is divided into three key components:

  • 🌊 Blue Water: Fresh surface and groundwater used for irrigation, livestock drinking water, and industrial processes.
  • 🌱 Green Water: Rainwater stored in the soil and absorbed by plants during growth (rain-fed agriculture).
  • 🏭 Grey Water: Water required to dilute pollutants (fertilizers, chemicals, waste) to safe levels.

Example: A cup of coffee (~200 L total) → 50 L Blue, 120 L Green, 30 L Grey. Knowing these categories helps us understand where water is being consumed or polluted, and how we can reduce our footprint through smarter choices.
